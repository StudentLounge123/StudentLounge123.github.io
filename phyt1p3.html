1.2 Scalars and Vectors: Speed, Velocity, Distance, Displacement and Acceleration

Introduction

In Physics, we classify physical quantities into 2 categories: scalar and vector

Scalar quantities have a magnitude which is a value but they don't need to be defined by direction. For example, you could say that the speed of the car is 25 metres per second but we do not need to state the direction of the car.

Vector quantities, on the other hand, have both magnitude and direction. For example, the velocity of the car is 25 metres per second and you have to state the direction of the car. So, the speed of the car is 25 metres per second while the velocity of the car is 25 metres per second to the East.

Scalar quantities include distance, speed, time, temperature, lengths, area, and energy. Vector quantities include displacement, velocity, acceleration, force, etc

Displacement and Distance:

The difference between distance and displacement is very important. The distance covered by a moving object is different from the displacement done to this object. To clarify more and have a better understanding, look at the photo below.

Basically, distance covered by a moving object is the length that this object has moved regardless of the direction while displacement is the shortest distance between the starting point and the ending point. The displacement is always a straight line and it has a direction. This makes the distance a scalar quantity and the displacement a vector quantity. An object moving in a straight line would have the same displacement and the same distance.
