Graphs: distance-time and speed-time

A very common way to illustrate the journey of a moving object is graphs. in this curriculum, you are required to know how to plot, draw, and interpret distance-time graphs as well as speed-time graphs.

• A distance-time graph would have the distance covered plotted on the Y-axis and the time taken plotted on the X-axis.

• Speed-time graph would have the speed of the object on the Y-axis and the time taken plotted on the X-axis

Velocity-time graphs are also important and you may be asked to interpret them. They are very similar to the speed time graphs. However, they have, on the Y-axis, negative values to indicate moving in the opposite direction.

How to interpret them?

• For distance-time graphs:

Calculating the slope of the line would give you the speed. Slope or gradient of the graph is calculated by drawing a right-angle triangle to the line (if it is straight) or to the tangent (if it is a curve). Rise÷run=gradient. (change in Y) ÷ (change in X) =Gradient.

    ✓ So, a straight line with a constant slope means that the object is moving at a constant uniform speed.
    ✓ So, a curve which is flattening means it has a decreasing slope indicating that the object is slowing down or decelerating.
    ✓ So, a curve which is growing exponentially has an increasing slope indicating that the object is speeding up or accelerating.
    ✓ So, a flat horizontal line means it has a slope of zero which indicates the object is not moving or at rest.

• Speed-time graphs:

Calculating the slope on this graph would give you the acceleration of the object. The acceleration is a vector quantity and it is defined as the change in velocity per unit time (how the velocity of the car changes relative to time). Deceleration is negative acceleration (it means object is slowing down // its velocity is decreasing)

    ✓ So, a straight line going upwards with a constant positive slope means that the speed of the object is increasing with the uniform acceleration. This means that the object’s speed increases by the same amount in the same time period.
    ✓ So, a straight line going downwards with a constant negative slope means that the object’s speed is decreasing and the deceleration is uniform. The speed decreases by the same amounts in the same time periods.
    ✓ So, a curve that is flattening with decreasing slope indicates the speed of the object is increasing with decreasing acceleration. This means that the object’s speed increases by a less amount in the same time period.
    ✓ So, A curve which is growing exponentially means the slope is increasing. This means the object’s speed increases by a greater amount in the same time period. This is increasing acceleration.
    ✓ So, a flat line means there is a slope of zero indication there is no acceleration and the speed is constant.
