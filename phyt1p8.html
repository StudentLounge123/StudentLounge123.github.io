1.6 Centre of Mass

    ➢ The centre of mass of an object (also referred to as centre of gravity) is the point at which the whole mass of the object appears to act
    ➢ The centre of mass in a geometrically-uniform object such as a cube or a sphere is actually is its geometric centre.
    ➢ For irregular objects where there is no geometric centre since they are geometrically-non-uniform, you do not need to learn how to identify the centre of mass
    ➢ However, you should be able to determine the centre of mass of a plane lamina using the following method.
    • You do not need to hang the plane lamina from 3 points. Two points is the minimum. The third point is actually for more accuracy.
    • Having a plum line ensures that the line marked is vertical and accurate.
    • The lines marked will intersect at a certain point. This point is the centre of mass of the lamina.

➢ The centre of mass and the shape of an object affects its stability:

    • The lower the centre of the mass, the more stable the object is and vice versa.
    • The wider the base of the object, the more stable it is and vice versa
