Time

Time is measured in seconds, minutes or hours. You can use stopwatch either a digital or an analogue. You should be able to read the time on stopwatch by knowing the minutes arm and the seconds arm.

You may use the stopwatch to measure the time of a race, one swing of the pendulum and other durations like, for example, a chemical reaction. Photos are shown below to help you understand how to take the reading on a stopwatch.

Potential Errors:

    • Human error due to reaction time is inevitable when measuring time. They can be reduced but they can never be eliminated.
    • The zero error [ the stopwatch should always be reset to 0 to prevent this error] .

Common questions regarding the practical paper:

Q: How to measure the thickness of a coin without having a micro metre screw gauge?

A: Bring 20 or more coins. Stack them on top of each other. Use a ruler and measure the height of 20 stacked coins. Look perpendicular to the scale of the ruler. Check for the zero error. Divide the reading you got by 20 to get the thickness of one coin. This makes it more accurate since the error is spread over 20 coins.

Q: How to measure the time of one swing of a pendulum[period] and reduce error as much as possible?

A: Set a stopwatch to zero. Measure the time for 20 swings. Divide the time taken over 20 to get the time for one swing only. This makes the error due to reaction time spread over 20 and this makes the reading more accurate. For reliability, repeat the procedure five times and take average.

Q: Why can’t we take the time for 200 swings instead of 20 swings even though the error would know be spread over a larger number of swings making it theoretically more accurate?

    • We would lose count and errors may occur due to confusion.
    • By 200 swings, the pendulum would probably stop swinging and the period will decrease as time goes on.
    • It will take a lot of time and it is not practical
