1.8 Pressure

➢ Pressure is a physical quantity which defines as force per unit area. From the definition, the equation for pressure is clearly:

    ➢ 𝑷𝒓𝒆𝒔𝒔𝒖𝒓𝒆 = 𝒇𝒐𝒓𝒄𝒆/ 𝒂𝒓𝒆a
    ➢ Subsequently, the unit for pressure is 𝑵/𝒎^𝟐 or 𝑵/𝒄𝒎^2
    ➢ From the equation, you can see that pressure is directly proportional to force. As force increases, the pressure increases and vice versa.
    ➢ You can also see that pressure and area are inversely proportional. When area increases, the pressure decreases and vice versa.
    ➢ This can relate to many different daily-life examples. For example, the nail has a pointed sharp head with a very small area. This is to increase the pressure applied to the walls so the nail can penetrate the wall easily.
    ➢ Another example is ice-skating use wide skate boards. This is to increase the area and reduce pressure applied to the thin ice. This to prevent the ice from breaking and person sinking into it.
